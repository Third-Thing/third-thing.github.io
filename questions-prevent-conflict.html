Introspective Questions Can Prevent Conflict

You can prevent conflict from even having a foundation by asking yourself the following questions.

Reflective Questions to Prevent Unnecessary Conflict

On motivation and purpose:

  • What am I hoping to accomplish by saying or doing this?
  • Is my goal to connect, to prove something, to defend myself, or to punish?
  • How likely is it that my goal will be achieved in the way I intend?

On emotional state:

  • What feelings am I noticing right now?
  • Am I acting out of defensiveness, anger, fear, or curiosity?
  • Do I feel an urge to “win” or to “understand”?

On assumptions:

  • Am I assuming we both mean the same thing by this term?
  • What am I assuming about their intentions—do I have evidence?
  • Could there be another explanation for their behavior?

On clarity:

  • Have I clearly stated what matters to me?
  • Do I know what matters to them? Have I asked?
  • Am I speaking from specific observations or vague generalizations?

On framing:

  • Is what I’m about to say likely to threaten their sense of identity, autonomy, or status?
  • Could I reframe my words to focus on the issue rather than the person?
  • Am I using loaded language or making unfavorable comparisons?

On epistemic humility:

  • How certain am I that my perspective is correct?
  • What’s the strongest argument against my position?
  • Could their position also seem coherent from their perspective?

Pocket Reflection: Before You Speak or Act

  1. What feelings am I noticing right now?
    • Am I defensive, angry, afraid, or curious?
  2. What's important to me here?
    • What needs, desires and priorities are involved?
  3. What am I hoping to accomplish?
    • Connection? Clarity? Defense? Punishment?
    • How likely is it that my goal will be achieved in the way I intend?
  4. What do I really know?
    • What evidence have I examined (for and against)?
    • What’s the strongest argument against my position?
  5. What might I be assuming?
    • Their intentions? Their meaning? Shared experience?
  6. What question could I ask instead of making a statement?
    • How can I show curiosity here?
